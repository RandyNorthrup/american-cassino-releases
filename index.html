About the Game

Capture, build, call, and trail your way to the winning score

American Cassino is a classic fishing-style card game played from a hand of cards against a shared table. Each turn asks you to decide whether to take points now, set up a stronger capture for later, or leave a card behind to change the shape of the table.

The heart of the game is table control. Players capture cards by matching values, combine cards into declared builds, call existing builds when they can claim them, or trail a card when they need to set up the next exchange.

Capture Take matching table cards or valid combinations with a card from your hand.
Build Group cards under a declared value so you can capture them on a later turn.
Call Claim control of a build when your hand can complete the capture.
Trail Play a card to the table when you are setting up future captures or blocking tempo.

Points come from smart collecting: aces, spades, key Cassino bonus cards, sweeps, and the total cards you gather all matter. Good play is about seeing the next few turns, protecting your builds, and denying your opponent the table they want.
